03 — the solution

What VitaLink does.

VitaLink pulls records, prescriptions, wellness data, and appointments into one app. Users see everything in one place instead of jumping between portals.

Why now

Three things changed recently that make an app like this possible.

  • Regulation: the 21st Century Cures Act now requires US providers to give patients open, FHIR-compliant access to their own records.
  • Consumer habits: Apple Health and Google Health Connect showed people are willing to track health data on their phone. Neither one aggregates medical records.
  • AI: language models can now summarize a stack of medical documents into something readable, which wasn't practical two years ago.

What we're not trying to do

VitaLink isn't a doctor. It doesn't diagnose, it doesn't give medical advice, and it doesn't replace the healthcare system. It just makes existing data easier to see and act on.

We also don't sell health data. That's not a marketing line — it's the model that keeps our incentives aligned with users. Revenue comes from subscriptions, not ads.

06 — who it's for

Who we're building for.

We identified three groups where the "scattered records" problem is worst, and where users are most likely to pay for a solution.

SA
Primary user

The caregiver adult

I'm keeping track of my kids' vaccines, my mom's cardiologist, and my own appointments. I want one place for all of it.

  • Aged 32–48, coordinating care for parents and kids
  • Most likely to pay for the Family plan
  • Finds proxy access setup on existing tools frustrating
CY
Growth driver

The young adult

I want to know what my body is doing. Sleep, workouts, why I feel off some weeks. Just tracking steps isn't enough.

  • Aged 18–29, already uses a wearable
  • More likely to share on social and refer friends
  • Cares about design and clear privacy policy
CC
Long-term retention

Adults with chronic conditions

I see multiple specialists and take several medications. I usually have to re-explain my history at every new appointment.

  • Adults managing two or more ongoing conditions
  • Highest lifetime value — the product becomes essential
  • Refers other patients they meet in support groups

08 — branding

Naming, logo, and colors.

We wanted the brand to feel trustworthy without feeling clinical — something people would actually want to open, not another portal they'd avoid.

The name

Vita comes from the Latin word for life. Link points to the product's function: connecting records, providers, and daily health data.

The tagline is "One link to better health." It's short, it fits on an app icon caption, and it says what the product does.

The logo

We tried three directions before picking one: a heart made from a link, a "VL" monogram, and a location pin with a medical cross. The heart concept was the only one that stayed readable at favicon size, so we went with it.

09 — how we built it

Our process.

The prototype in front of you took roughly four stages. Here's what each one covered.

Research

Interviews with people who deal with scattered health data. The three personas in section 5 came out of those conversations.

Design

Three logo options, a six-color palette, and grayscale wireframes for each screen before adding color.

Build

Built with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Served on a small Node server so it runs locally without any installation.

Test

Checked every page on desktop and mobile screen sizes and adjusted the layout where things broke.
